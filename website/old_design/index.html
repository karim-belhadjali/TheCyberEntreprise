Tokens
Why create a token?

The concept behind Cyber Enterprise was to create a token without expiration, solidified on the blockchain. A token without a blockchain, is something merely impersonating what a true token is. The blockchain is what provides credibility to investors, amongst other things. To create a series of decentralized applications is what our idea is all about. We didn’t want to create a token with a low supply, and therefore created a token with limited supply. There will never be more of this token on the market.

What is a token?

Token can be viewed as a digital asset that represents value, but aren’t inherent value themselves. In the case of the Cyber (CYBR) Token, it can as of right now be exchanged for ethereum, and thus, or can be used with the ever growing and developing Cyber Enterprise ecosystem.

How does a token work?

A token launched on a decentralized exchange such as UniSwap follows an Automated Market Model (AMM), which means all trading is done on the blockchain and trading can never be turned off as long as there’s liquidity. Token without liquidity is useless because funds in ETH cannot be withdrawn, and isn't the token to buy now.

Can the token be mined?

In our case the Cyber Token can not be a minable asset as it was not created this way. An initial supply of one Quadrillion was minted at the start. The token can now be purchased on UniSwap V3, won through giveaways, or earned through our future reward systems such as locking. The CYBR token to USD pair is not available at this time, but rather Ethereum is needed. This can be bought with USD through a centralized exchange.

Can token money be refunded?

The token can always be refunded for Ethereum using one of the supported exchanges. You have the ability to trade freely, even if it may be the token to the moon.

Token vs Cryptocurrency?

Token is not defined as a cryptocurrency, because a token does not represent the underlying value of a debatable asset, but rather a direct medium of asset or service exchange.

Token vs NFT

The token we are using is an ERC-20, rather than an NFT which is an ETC-721 or ERC-1155. The major difference is that ERC-20 tokens are fungible, meaning that the asset has the ability to be interchanged for another of that kind, working within that contract. They are divisible and none unique, retaining the same value.

Smart Contract
Why is a smart contract important?

Our smart contracts are written in Solidity, which is native to the Ethereum blockchain. They are defined algorithms that establish parameters for transactions. If the parameters of the contract are not fulfilled the contract will not be executed.

What is a Contract Address on Ethereum?

The contact address is the outcome of a hash algorithm that represents the smart contract with a unique code, where it can be publicly viewed on the Ethereum blockchain.

How does our smart contract work?

Unlike a NFT smart contract, we do not govern the rules of minting an nft, but rather trading our native token. Our smart contract has measures for inflation, bot mitigation and maintenance along with many more functions.

Max Supply
What is the max supply in cryptocurrency?

The max supply in crypto is the maximum amount of tokens that will ever exist in that cryptocurrency lifetime. In the specific case of the Cyber (CYBR), the max supply is equivalent to the initial supply which is 1 Quadrillion (1,000,000,000,000,000) tokens, and as there is no minting there will never be more.

How to calculate max supply?

The max supply of a digital currency can be viewed on Etherscan, thus no calculation is needed as this number will never change. Within Etherscan you can also locate when the initial supply was made, the transaction hash, and the creator wallet that minted the tokens.

Circulating Tokens
Is circulating supply important for cryptocurrency?

The reason why a circulating supply is important is because it refers to the number of tokens that are publicly available and circulating both on the market as well as in the hands of holders.

Can a circulating supply decrease?

The circulating supply in cryptocurrency can decrease if a burn mechanism is implemented. Based on our 2% buy tax tokenomics, or RUBBE system initiatives, Cyber (CYBR) is sent to a dead wallet that cannot sell the token, which is hard coded within the contract.

How does circulating supply affect a cryptocurrency?

Circulating supply and price have an indirect correlation with one another. Typically, the circulating supply of a cryptocurrency may increase or decrease over a period of time, however, Cybers (CYBR) circulating supply will gradually decrease due to implemented burn mechanisms set out to gradually reduce the amount of tokens available on the market.

Circulating supply vs Max supply

Circulating supply refers to tokens that are readily available on the market at any given time whereas max supply refers to all the tokens that will ever be in existence.

Pooled Tokens
What is a pooled token?

A token within a token pool are crowdsourced funds of cryptocurrencies or tokens that are locked within a smart contract. They are used to facilitate trade between assets on a decentralized exchange (DEX). The pool of is the true amount readily available on the that exchange, for example UniSwap V3, ShibaSwap, or our future CyberSwap.

How do pooled tokens work?

When a token smart contract is launched, tokens are created and added to a decentralized exchange. Any individual can also become a liquidity provider and earn their own rewards by adding a token to a pool, and the contract will return a “liquidity pool token” representing their stake within the pool. This acts as a share of the fees paid by traders who use the pool, which has a proportional value to their staked liquidity.

Liquidity provider meaning?

A liquidity provider in crypto is someone a market maker responsible for enhancing the liquidity in the trading platform. This is done by making a pair with both Ethereum, and the native token, enabling the user to gain profits from other buying and selling the digital asset.

Burning
Why burn Tokens?

Burning tokens can be explained as reducing the supply over time,while also providing incentives for proof of stake rewards, to benefit the users of The Cyber Enterprise ecosystem.

How does token burn work?

How tokens burn in crypto are through implementing an algorithm which uses transaction fees through smart contracts and allocating them to be burned.

Is token burn good?

In the long run, token burns are good, as it supports an asset's price and as well as regulating a tokens supply. It is important that the burns takes place after the liquidity pool is made, as it can create false illusion. For example, if a token states that 10% of the supply goes to the Team wallet, and then they burn 50% of the supply, and put 40 % on the market, the Teams holding is now 20% of the circulating supply.
